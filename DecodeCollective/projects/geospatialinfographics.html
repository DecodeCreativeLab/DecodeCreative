Geospatial Analytics To Data Driven Design

Perceptive Datascapes

This research reflects upon principles of physical connectivity and visual perception as the means to evaluate and generate new spatial configurations for contemporary urban systems. The aim is to explore the possibility of constructing a computational analytic model, termed “Perceptive Machine”, which could function as an additional layer in the design process by mining, analysing and visualising geo-spatial data in a virtual 3D space. This system helps in the creation of new topographies which encourage the interplay between human and inhuman intelligence in an environment of hybrid cyberspace. Hypervirtuality, the analysis and evaluation of a set of multidimensional continuous data in Virtual Reality could challenge the way people perceive the urban fabric. This immersive space that mixes real and virtual offers the necessary ground for this analytic workflow to expand beyond computation into a gradient of experiences.
