Automated Design and Object Recognition

Rawbot

This collaborative research project is aiming to utilise immersive technologies in the design and fabrication process, for the benefit of people living in rural areas of the devoloping world. Technology infrastucture in these areas is sparse, so Rawbot consolidates and automates the design and assembly instructions for fast and affordable bamboo housing in a single smartphone app. Living spaces are allocated by the user and the bamboo poles get automatically placed to shape the structure. As the poles are being harvested on site and are not undergoing any standardisation treatment, they will be used with all their irregularities. For that reason, Machine Learning is called to "scan" the material real-time, without using extra equipment such as a 3D scanner. A neural network has been trainned to detect the start, end and nodes of bamboo sticks. This way Rawbot is using the data to construct a discrete yet raw material and pass it on as abstract geometry to the assembly stage. The user interacts with Rawbot through an Augmented Reality (AR) client that projects in 3D space the final postion of the piece. Special silicone/steel core joints, were developed to keep the pieces at, predictable, right angles, while their adjustable size can respond to range of bamboo thicknesses.
